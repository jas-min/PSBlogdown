This analysis is going to explore some of the different circumstances behind fatal police shootings in where the victim was fleeing.

Type of Fleeing

Figure 1 below shows the proportion of those who do not flee, who do, and if they are fleeing, what method of fleeing that these individuals are using.

#proportion are killed while attempting to flee
shootings %>%
  mutate(count = 1) %>%
  aggregate(count ~ flee + year, data = ., sum) %>%
  group_by(year) %>%
  mutate(proportion = count / sum(count)) %>%
  ggplot() +
    geom_line(mapping = aes(x = year, y = proportion, color = flee)) +
    ggtitle("Proportion of Fatal Police Shooting by Flee Method") + 
    xlab("Year") + ylab("Proportion") + theme_bw()

Figure 1. The proportion of fatal police shootings by flee method over time.

Figure 1 depicts the proportion of fleeing by car decreasing and every other method is seen increasing, in recent. The proportion of the incidents where fleeing method is not recorded is increasing every year, almost to the same degree that the “Not Fleeing” method is decreasing, this should be taken into account.

unarmed <- shootings %>%
